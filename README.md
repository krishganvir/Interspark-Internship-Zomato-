# Interspark-Internship-Zomato

Zomato Market Opportunity & Feature Strategy
Data Overview Method: Regular Expressions (Regex) were deployed to cleanly extract
decimal ratings from corrupted text fields. Data was mapped into 'Low', 'Medium', and 'High'
tiers using standard boxplots and pastel frequency visualizations to determine cost-to-rating
elasticity.
Market Insights
•The Premium Price Variance: Boxplot distributions prove that while low-rated
restaurants have a strict price ceiling, "High" rated restaurants have massive cost
elasticity—ranging from highly affordable to absolute luxury.
•Secondary Hub Profitability: While BTM leads in gross volume, secondary tech-hubs
like Whitefield maintain highly profitable, structured restaurant counts.
•Convenience is King: Traditional dining is statistically overshadowed by "Delivery"
and "Quick Bites" formats.
Proposed Initiatives for Alfido Tech
•Initiative 1 (Menu visual mandates): Because highly-rated restaurants often push
higher prices, the platform must mandate high-resolution imagery for all items over
₹500 to justify the consumer expense.
•Initiative 2 (Surge & drop pricing): Implement dynamic delivery fees. Drop the
delivery fees in high-density areas during mid-afternoon slumps to drive volume
when restaurants are traditionally empty.
•Initiative 3 (Weighted algorithmic sorting): A 4.5 rating from 10 people is statistically
useless compared to a 4.2 from 1,000 people. Our search algorithm must weigh total
vote count over the raw rating.
•Initiative 4 (B2B SaaS Dashboard): Develop a merchant-facing analytics portal.
Charge restaurant owners a small monthly fee to see exactly where their pricing sits
compared to local block competitors.
•Initiative 5 (Corporate micro-hubs): Shift B2B marketing toward secondary tech hubs
like Whitefield to lock down bulk corporate-lunch deliveries.
